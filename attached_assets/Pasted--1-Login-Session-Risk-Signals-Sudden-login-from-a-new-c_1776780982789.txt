🔐 1. Login & Session Risk Signals
Sudden login from a new country or region
IP changes within a short time window
Use of VPNs, proxies, or datacenter IP ranges
Logging in from a device/browser never seen before
Session anomalies (invalid tokens, rapid reconnects, unusual handshake patterns)
Multiple concurrent sessions from different locations

🌐 2. IP Reputation & Network Layer
IP address previously associated with spam/scam activity
Known VPN endpoints or hosting providers (AWS, Azure, etc.)
Shared IP used by many Discord accounts (account farms)
Rapid IP rotation (proxy switching)
ISP vs datacenter mismatch patterns

🖥️ 3. Device & Client Fingerprinting
Browser fingerprint (user agent, OS, screen size, rendering quirks)
Cookie/session inconsistencies
Multiple accounts sharing the same fingerprint
Suspicious client builds (modified clients, unofficial apps)
Missing or abnormal telemetry expected from official clients

⚙️ 4. Automation / Self-bot Detection
Perfectly consistent timing between actions
Actions occurring faster than human reaction speed
No typing indicators before messages
API usage patterns that differ from official clients
Repeated request signatures or headers
Direct API calls without proper client flow
Long-running scripted sessions

💬 5. Messaging Behavior Analysis
Sending identical or near-identical messages repeatedly
High volume of DMs in a short time
Messaging many non-friends quickly
Low reply ratio (sending a lot, receiving little)
Links or content matching known spam/scam patterns
Copy-paste patterns across servers

👥 6. Social Graph & Interaction Signals
Interacting with flagged or reported accounts
Being part of servers known for spam/scams
Receiving reports from other users
Rapid friend requests or unsolicited contacts
Low mutual connections with message targets

🧾 7. Account History & Trust Score
Newly created account (low baseline trust)
Sudden change in behavior (quiet → high activity)
Previous warnings, flags, or restrictions
Email/phone verification status
Incomplete profile setup

🔗 8. Server & Invite Activity
Joining many servers quickly
Using suspicious or mass-distributed invite links
Joining servers already flagged by Discord
Join → message → leave patterns

📊 9. Rate Limits & Threshold Systems
Exceeding message rate limits
Triggering DM anti-spam thresholds
Too many actions (reactions, edits, joins) in short bursts
Hitting hidden behavioral thresholds (not publicly defined)

🧠 10. Machine Learning / Pattern Matching
Behavioral similarity to known spam/bot accounts
Clustering with previously banned accounts
Content similarity models (scam phrases, templates)
Long-term pattern tracking across sessions

🔄 11. Token / Authentication Signals
Token reuse across multiple IPs/devices
Token appearing in known leaks or logs
Invalid or manipulated authentication flows
Rapid token refresh or abnormal auth cycles

📡 12. Client Integrity Checks
Differences between expected vs actual client behavior
Missing events normally sent by official Discord clients
Unusual sequencing of actions (e.g., sending before ready state)

⚠️ 13. Passive Risk (No Activity Needed)
Even without sending messages:
High-risk IP + new device combo
Suspicious client connection pattern
Association with flagged accounts
Login anomalies alone 
🧩 14. Content & Language Analysis
Messages matching known scam/phishing templates
Repeated structure with slight variations (e.g. same sentence, small edits)
High ratio of links vs normal conversation
Use of shortened/obfuscated URLs
Language patterns typical of spam campaigns (e.g. urgency, giveaways, “click now”)

🕒 15. Temporal Behavior Profiling
Activity at perfectly regular intervals over long periods
No natural “human pauses” (sleep, idle gaps, random delays)
Continuous 24/7 uptime without breaks
Immediate response timing regardless of context

🔁 16. Behavioral Consistency Checks
Account suddenly switching from normal chatting → automated patterns
Inconsistent behavior between sessions (human one day, bot-like next)
Identical behavior repeated across multiple days/sessions

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 17. Cross-Account Correlation
Multiple accounts showing identical timing and message patterns
Accounts created in batches behaving similarly
Same device/IP interacting across many accounts
“Mirrored” activity (same actions across accounts within seconds)

📥 18. Recipient Reaction Signals
High ignore rate (people don’t reply)
Users blocking or reporting your account
Messages getting deleted by mods or auto-moderation bots
Low engagement vs high outbound activity

🏷️ 19. Server-Level Signals
Servers with strict anti-spam bots flagging your messages
Being muted/kicked/banned across multiple servers
Posting in channels where you have no prior interaction
Violating server-specific rate or content rules

📈 20. Trust Decay Over Time
Repeated minor suspicious signals stacking up
Gradual lowering of trust score even without a single major violation
Older flags resurfacing when new suspicious behavior appears

🔍 21. Heuristic “Red Flags”
Sending messages immediately after joining a server
Messaging users without shared history
Very short session durations with high activity bursts
Repetitive navigation patterns (join → DM → leave)

🔗 22. External Link Intelligence
Links pointing to domains previously flagged
Newly registered domains (common in scams)
Domains shared across many flagged accounts
Redirect chains that hide final destinations

🧪 23. Experimentation / A-B Detection Systems
Discord testing different thresholds on different users
Some accounts being more sensitive to triggers
Gradual tightening of limits based on behavior
